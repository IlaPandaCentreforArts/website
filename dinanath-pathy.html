Late Dr. Dinanath Pathy
Founder Chairperson, IPCA Managing Committee

A distinguished painter, visionary and pioneer of the modern and contemporary art movement in Odisha, Dr. Dinanath Pathy was brought up in a family of artists and poets in the traditional town of Digapahandi, Ganjam in Odisha.

Dr. Pathy was the former secretary of Lalit Kala Akademi in New Delhi and Bhubaneswar and the founding Principal of the Bibhuti Kanungo College of Art and Crafts, Bhubaneshwar. He was nominated for the International Rietberg Award from Rietberg Society in 2014 for his profound contributions to research on Indian art history. He authored over 50 books in English, Odia and German on classical, traditional, tribal, folk, rural and contemporary art of Odisha.

His works have been recognized at national and international levels and earned a number of awards, fellowships and scholarships such as President of India Silver Plaque for Painting from All India Fine Art and Crafts Society (1988); Odisha Sahitya Academy Award for autobiography "Digapahandira Drawing Master", and Jawaharlal Nehru Fellowship for his research on art. Known as the pioneer of art movement in Odisha, Dr. Pathy was instrumental in taking Odisha's art to the global platform through his paintings, writings and teachings. His demise at the age of 74, in 2016, has created a vacuum in the art world of Odisha.
