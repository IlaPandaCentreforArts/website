Art Residency

Vignettes: A Portraiture Residency

IPCA's Youth in Art programme invites young emerging artists to explore portraiture this season, in a six week residency programme during August-September 2018.
This residency is open to young emerging artists of Odisha who have started their painting practice in the last 3-4 years and are interested in working on portraiture. A very important discipline in visual art, painting portraits has not been getting due attention in recent times.With the objective of rejuvenating interest and skills in portraiture painting especially among young emerging artists, the residency programme will focus on painting contemporary portraits from life.

Note: Selected female applicants (if any) can avail of the residency facilities during the working days, but IPCA will not be able to provide accommodation for them at the residency.


Important Dates for IPCA Portraiture Residency

Application: 19 July 2018 to 26 July 2018
Intimation to Selected artists: 27 July 2018
Residency duration: 01 August 2018 to 13 Sept 2018
Interested candidates need to fill up online form here, and mail good quality images of the following items to prabal.mallick@gmail.com immediately after submitting the form. Please note that the file size of each image should not exceed 1MB.
1. Your recent passport size photograph
2. Recent sketches (for judging drawing skills) – 5
3. Recent paintings (for judging medium proficiency) - 3
4. ID proof (Aadhar/PAN/Student Identity Card)



Terms and Conditions

  1. RESIDENCY PURPOSE: The purpose of this art residency at IPCA is to provide space and guidance to young artists to hone their skills in the field of portrait painting from life. The residency is open to young emerging artists of Odisha who have started their painting practice in the last 3-4 years and are interested in working on portraiture. IPCA programmes are based on policies of non-discrimination and equal opportunity for people regardless of sex, race, religion and creed. The following facilities are included in the residency programme:
    a. Accommodation
    b. Food
    c. Art materials
    d. Studio space
    e. Mentoring and guidance by professionals

  2. TIMING: Residency programme will be run from 01 August 2018 to 13 September 2018. Any changes in the dates will be intimated well in advance to all stake holders.

  3. TECHNIQUE: Painting: Artist must be comfortable working with the following medium. Oil, watercolour, acrylic and charcoal.
  1. SELECTION: Each artist will have to submit images of few of their art works as requested for carrying out selection procedure. The first round selection of the submitted art works will be carried out by a jury made up of members consisting of established artists. The second and final round of selections will be carried out by IPCA through personal interviews. The Jury and IPCA’s decisions are irrevocable, unquestionable, not open to appeal, unchallengeable and final. A certain number of artists will be selected in reserve in case of other artists’ missing participation. The selection criteria for the first round are given below. But it is not limited to these criteria.
    a. Draughtsmanship (Drawing ability)
    b. Handling of oil/watercolour/acrylic medium
    c. Artistic Potential
  1. FINANCES: The following costs will be borne by IPCA
    a. Accommodation at IPCA art residency
    b. Food (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner)
    c. Art Materials: IPCA will provide standard art material. If the artist uses a particular brand which IPCA does not provide then the artist will procure that on his own. IPCA will not be reimbursing the expenses. IPCA will not provide brushes and palettes to the residents. As brushes and palettes are personal tools it is assumed that the participants will bring their own brushes.
    d. Studio space
    e. Fees of experts providing guidance to participants
    Any other costs which are not mentioned above will be borne by the artists.
  1. APPLICATION METHOD: The candidates must submit their application forms by 26 July 2018. The candidates must fill in each requested field of the application form. They need to fill up the online form given on IPCA website. The candidates must keep a copy of the registered document sent by mail. On completion of form candidates have to send images of their art works as requested.

Any insurance will be supported by the artist himself. Consequently, the responsibility of the art works, and any damage to them, will be totally borne by the artist, who assumes all risks and declares he/she is aware that IPCA will not provide any compensation in case of theft or damage of the art works.

  3. HOUSE RULES
    a. Artists are not allowed to host anyone at IPCA compound.
    b. Visitors are allowed only during the day with consent from IPCA management.
    c. Artists can go out in their free time, but should be back at IPCA before 8:00 PM in the evening.
    d. Absence during the residency programme will be allowed only through written consent.
    e. It is forbidden to use any intoxicant including and not limited to alcohol, cigarette and drugs during the IPCA Art Residency.
    f. Expenses for any damages to IPCA property caused by the artist during the residency will be borne by the artist.

  4. AGREEMENT: IPCA will keep one art work from each artist for its collection at the end of the residency programme. Each artist gives IPCA the rights of reproduction, exhibition, publication, translation and communication, in any manner and without exception. Fully respecting the moral right of the author, IPCA is allowed to use the artist’s image for activities and diffusion.
